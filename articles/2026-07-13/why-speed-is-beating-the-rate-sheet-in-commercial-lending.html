Why Speed Is Beating the Rate Sheet in Commercial Lending

13 July 2026 · synthesised from 2 sources

A June 2026 broker survey shows ANZ and Westpac topping usage share in commercial lending — but the numbers that actually decide a deal are turnaround time and credit-assessment satisfaction, not brand habit.

Agile Market Intelligence surveyed 130 active commercial brokers between 1 and 25 June 2026 and found ANZ leads broker usage for commercial mortgages at 30%, ahead of NAB on 20% and CBA on 19%. ANZ tops business loans too, at 23%, with NAB on 20% and CBA on 15%. In asset finance, Westpac's 32% barely clears non-bank lenders Angle Finance (30%) and Metro Finance (28%). If you only read the headline numbers, the majors look like the obvious panel default.

Turnaround data from the same survey tells a different story. Prospa settles commercial mortgages in 3.2 business days on average, Judo Bank in 3.9 — both non-banks, both well inside the multi-week timeframes commercial credit teams at the majors are typically still running. On business loans, OnDeck turns files around in 0.7 days and Bizcap in 0.9. Service quality splits the same way: ING scores 100% broker satisfaction on BDM support and Flexi Commercial 97% on credit assessment, against 88% for the major banks on the same measure. Usage share reflects habit and relationship history; it doesn't reflect who actually gets a file to "yes" fastest.

Build a panel matrix, not a favourites list

This is the gap panel-lender selection is meant to close: choosing which lenders sit on your panel — and which one you recommend for a given deal — by scoring product fit, turnaround time and service quality against each other, rather than defaulting to whichever major you've always used. In practice that's a simple table: lender, product, typical turnaround, satisfaction score, and any policy quirks (LVR caps, security types accepted, doc requirements). It takes an afternoon to build and it's worth revisiting every time a new survey cycle like Agile Market Intelligence's lands, because the rankings move — Angle Finance and Metro Finance closing to within four points of Westpac in asset finance is a newer development, not a stable fact.

The second, related habit is loan-turnaround benchmarking: tracking actual days-to-settlement per lender per product and treating that number as a live input into the recommendation, not background trivia. Say a commercial client needs equipment finance settled inside five business days to catch a supplier contract deadline. OnDeck's 0.7-day average clears that with more than four days to spare; a major bank business-loan process running two to three weeks doesn't get close. The client doesn't care about your panel's brand names — they care whether the equipment arrives on time. A broker who's tracked the turnaround numbers recommends the right lender in one conversation; a broker working off habit finds out too late that the "safe" choice cost the deal.

Why this matters more right now

Broker Daily reported on 7 July that Finni is seeing record inquiry volumes as SMSF clients rush to lock in residential borrowing before the limited recourse borrowing arrangement window closes to new borrowers on 10 August. That deadline is compressing timeframes across a broker's whole pipeline, not just SMSF files — every extra day spent on a slow lender for an unrelated commercial deal is a day not spent clearing the SMSF queue before it shuts. A panel matrix with real turnaround numbers is what lets you triage: which files can sit with a slower, cheaper major, and which need the non-bank that settles in under a week.

Check the numbers before you recommend

None of this means drop the majors — ANZ and Westpac's usage share exists because they're genuinely competitive on plenty of deals, and CBA and NAB still clear plenty of files well inside a client's timeframe. It means the recommendation should follow the data for the specific product and client situation, not the lender you reached for last time. Before your next commercial or SME referral, pull whatever turnaround and satisfaction numbers you can find for the two or three lenders you're weighing — Broker Daily and aggregator BDMs both publish this kind of survey data periodically — and let the deadline the client is actually working to decide the recommendation.
