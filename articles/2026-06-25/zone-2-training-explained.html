Zone 2 training, explained

25 June 2026 · synthesised from 2 sources

The slow, conversational pace that builds your aerobic base — what Zone 2 is, why it works, and how to find yours.

Zone 2 is the effort level where you're working, but could still hold a conversation in full sentences. In heart-rate terms it sits roughly at 60–70% of your maximum — easy enough that it feels almost too gentle to be useful.

That gentleness is the point. At this intensity your body relies on fat as its main fuel and trains the mitochondria — the cells' aerobic engines — to become more numerous and efficient. Push harder and you tip into burning mostly glucose and accumulating fatigue; stay in Zone 2 and you can accumulate a lot of training time with little stress, which is what actually grows your aerobic base.

The practical recipe is unglamorous: most of your easy cardio (think 80% of it) should be slow enough to pass the talk test, with only a small slice spent going genuinely hard. The most common mistake is going too fast on easy days, which leaves you too tired to go hard on the hard days — and stuck in an exhausting middle that builds neither.
